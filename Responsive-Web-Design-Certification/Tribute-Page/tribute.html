About Jane Austen

Jane Austen (1775-1817) was an English novelist known primarily for her six major novels, which include Pride and Prejudice, Sense and Sensibility, and Emma. Her novels often critiqued the manners and customs of the British landed gentry of the end of the 18th century. Austen's plots also explore the dependence of women on marriage in the pursuit of economic security. Her stories portray headstrong women who manage to find a marriage partner they love and who does not risk their financial security. Her use of biting irony, along with her realism and social commentary, have earned her acclaim among critics and scholars.

Published Novels

  • Sense and Sensibility (1811)
  • Pride and Prejudice (1813)
  • Mansfield Park (1814)
  • Emma (1815)
  • Northanger Abbey (1818, posthumous)
  • Persuasion (1818, posthumous)

Timeline of Her Life

  • 1775 - Jane Austen is born on December 16 in Steventon, Hampshire, England
  • 1783 - Austen and her sister Cassandra are sent to Oxford to be educated
  • 1787 - Austen begins her formal education at boarding school
  • 1790 - Austen writes her first juvenile works, including *Love and Freindship*
  • 1796 - Austen begins writing her first major novel, initially titled First Impressions, which later becomes Pride and Prejudice
  • 1801 - The Austen family moves to Bath
  • 1805 - Austen's father dies
  • 1809 - The Austen women move to Chawton where Jane lived the rest of her life
  • 1811 - Austen begins publishing her novels with Sense and Sensibility
  • 1817 - Austen dies on July 18th in Winchester at age 41
